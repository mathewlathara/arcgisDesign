Dataset

Combining datasets

Land cover (MODIS 2001-2019; TRCA/CLOCA current), soils (Ontario Detailed Soil Survey), Statistics Canada census tract (1996 2001. 2006, 2011, 2016), and ECCC weather (2000- ZU20) data were combined with the water quality data and the sampling site's respective drainage basin.

MODIS

The drainage basin is used to clip the MODIS land cover raster (250 m resolution). The remaining pixels are summed to give a count of each land cover toe for the given drainage basin. This clippina is done for each of the 19 MODIS land cover rasters (2001-2019), to provide the relative area of each land cover type tor each drainage basin over the 19-year period.

TRCA/CLOCA

The IRCA/CLUCA land use shape files are merged into a single shaperile, and subsequently are converted to a single raster tile with a resolution or 10m. The land use/cover categories are recorded as unique pixel values. This raster file is clipped using the drainage basin and again the relative area of each land cover type is recorded.

Detailed Soil Survey

The Detailed Soil Survey shape file is rasterized to a resolution of 10m. The shapefile is accompanied with various additional attributes tiles containing information about sol components and sol layers These data are combined to calculate weighted means based on relative component areas and soil deon lavers - over the entire depth of the recorded soil column. The values we have extracted are: percent clay, silt, sand, other and unknown, and saturated hydraulic conductivity. Again, these rasters are clipped using the drainage basin and the resulting values are representative of the mean in the entire drainage basin for the entire recorded soil depth.

Statistics Canada

To determine the socioeconomic influence on the water quality in the drainage basin, we used Statistics Canada Census data at the highest spatial resolution Census Tract (CT) scale. Onlv those CTs that intersect the drainage basin are included. The CT-level data (e.g., Dopulation. dwellinas. emplovment) for all CTs within the drainage basin are aggregated using data from the closest Census year. I.e., a 2005 collection date uses data from the 2006 Census.
